MOTIVATION

Why I Built This

Freelancers and solo business owners face a painful paradox — enterprise tools are too bloated for a one-person team, but simple spreadsheets lack the integrity needed to manage a professional workflow.

Our team of three set out to build a unified platform covering the full project lifecycle — from initial lead to final paid invoice. I took ownership of the backend and infrastructure, building a robust, secure, high-performance engine to power the entire system.

APPROACH

The Solution

Not just a database — an automated business partner. The system was architected around a strict project lifecycle so that managing a business feels as professional as the work itself.

  1. State-Driven Workflow A system of Enums strictly manages customer, project, and task status — ensuring every project moves through a predictable lifecycle from new lead to completed invoice.
  2. Revenue Bridge A dynamic invoicing system links directly to project milestones, keeping all financial data in sync with project progress.
  3. Actionable Analytics Data synthesized across projects and clients surfaces insights that would otherwise be buried in disorganised files.
MODULE 01 — DASHBOARD

A Dynamic Engine of Insight

As the backend developer, my goal was to ensure the dashboard wasn't just static numbers — I architected the controller logic to aggregate complex relational data into a unified, actionable display.

  • Integrated Business Metrics Controller logic that instantly calculates and serves high-impact KPIs — Active Projects, Pending Tasks, and Unpaid Invoices.
  • Intelligent Prioritisation Backend queries that automatically flag recent client interactions and upcoming deadlines, drawing attention to items that require urgent action.
  • Lifecycle Awareness By hooking into the Project and Task Enums, the dashboard dynamically reflects the current state of the entire business pipeline.

MODULE 02 — ANALYTICS

12 Months of Business Health at a Glance

A solo business owner gets lost in the day-to-day grind and loses sight of the bigger picture. I architected the analytics engine to transform raw data into a clear narrative of business growth.

  • Three-Timeline Growth Queries that automatically calculate all-time totals, last-month performance, and last-year benchmarks — so the owner can compare current pace to historical average instantly.
  • 12-Month Trend Reconstruction A complex iterative loop groups customer acquisitions, project completions, and task velocity by month — producing a visual trendline of business growth over the past year.
  • 360-Degree Data Synthesis The engine pulls from every core model — Customers, Projects, and Tasks — ensuring a complete, unified view of business operations.

MODULE 03 — CUSTOMERS

Store Context, Not Just Contacts

A contact list isn't enough for a freelancer. You need a system that understands the nuance of professional relationships — distinguishing a warm lead from a long-term partner and tracking every conversation in between.

  • Enum-Driven Categorisation Customers categorised by Industry, Type, and Status using a suite of Enums — allowing the owner to filter their client base with surgical precision.
  • Interaction History A dedicated Interaction model linked to each customer creates a paper trail of every email, call, and meeting — a complete relationship history at a glance.
  • Financial Footprint Customers bridge directly to their Projects and Invoices — so a customer record shows not just a contact, but their entire operational and financial history.
